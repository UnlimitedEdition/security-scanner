      Kako da zastitis svoj prvi sajt

      Kompletan vodic za potpune pocetnike — od nule do zasticenog sajta, bez ikakvih tehnickih predznanja

      1. Zasto je bezbednost vazna — cak i za mali sajt

      Ako ste upravo napravili svoj prvi sajt — portfolio, blog, mali biznis — mozda mislite: “Ko bi napao moj sajt? Imam samo par posetilaca.”

      Odgovor je iznenadjujuci: automatski programi (botovi). Zamislite to ovako — zamislite da postoji robot koji luta ulicom i proba da otvori svaka vrata. Njemu je svejedno da li je u pitanju vila ili garsonjerka. Ako su vrata otvorena, ulazi. Isto rade botovi na internetu — skeniraju milione sajtova dnevno trazeci otvorena “vrata” (poznate ranjivosti).

      Sta se desava kada bot nadje nezasticen sajt?

      • Google vas oznaci kao opasan sajt — posetioci vide crveno upozorenje pre nego sto mogu da pristupe. Vas SEO rang (pozicija na Google-u) pada drasticno, ponekad nepovratno.
      • Sajt se koristi za spam — napadaci ubace nevidljive linkove ka laznim prodavnicama ili malicioznim sajtovima. Vasi posetioci to ne vide, ali Google jeste.
      • Kradja podataka — ako imate kontakt formu, korisnicke naloge ili bilo kakve licne podatke, sve to moze biti ukradeno i zloupotrebljeno.
      • Gubitak reputacije — jednom kada posetioci saznaju da je vas sajt bio hakovan, tesko je vratiti poverenje. To je kao da restoran dobije losu sanitarnu inspekciju — cak i posle popravke, ljudi pamte.
      • Troskovi oporavka — popravka hakovanog sajta moze kostati stotine evra i dane rada. Prevencija je uvek jeftinija od lecenja.
      Dobra vest: Osnovna zastita je potpuno besplatna i ne zahteva programersko znanje. Ovaj vodic pokriva sve sto vam treba — korak po korak, sa objasnjenjima na srpskom.

      Vizuelni tok zastite vaseg sajta:

      Napravi sajt → Skeniraj → Popravi probleme → Skeniraj ponovo → Objavi sigurno

      Ovaj vodic prati upravo ovaj tok. Na kraju cete znati tacno sta da uradite i kojim redom.

      2. HTTPS i SSL — zakljucana koverta za vas sajt

      Zamislite da saljete pismo. Mozete ga poslati na otvorenom razglednicom — svako ko drzi pismo moze da procita sadrzaj. Ili mozete da ga stavite u zakljucanu kovertu koju moze da otvori samo primalac. HTTPS je ta zakljucana koverta za vas sajt.

      Sta je SSL/HTTPS jednostavnim recima:

      • HTTP (bez “S”) = razglednica. Svako na mrezi (vasa Wi-Fi mreza, internet provajder, hakeri na javnom Wi-Fi-ju) moze da vidi sta vasi posetioci rade na vasem sajtu.
      • HTTPS (sa “S” = Secure/Sigurno) = zakljucana koverta. Komunikacija je enkriptovana (kodirana) tako da samo posetilac i vas server mogu da je procitaju.
      • SSL sertifikat = digitalna potvrda identiteta vaseg sajta. Kao licna karta za sajt — dokazuje da ste vi zaista vi, a ne neko ko se pretvara.

      Kako prepoznajete HTTPS: Pogledajte address bar (traku gde pisite web adresu). Ako vidite mali katanac i adresa pocinje sa https://, vas sajt je zasticen. Ako vidite “Not Secure” ili “Nije bezbedno”, HTTPS nije aktivan.

      Kako da dobijete HTTPS besplatno (po platformi):

      • Vercel — automatski. Cim postavite sajt na Vercel, HTTPS je vec aktivan. Ne morate nista raditi.
      • Netlify — automatski. Ista prica — deploy-ujte sajt i HTTPS je tu.
      • GitHub Pages — idite u Settings → Pages → cekirajte “Enforce HTTPS”. Gotovo za 5 minuta.
      • WordPress — vecina hosting provajdera (SiteGround, Bluehost, itd.) nudi besplatan SSL sa jednim klikom u cPanel-u. Potrazite “SSL/TLS” ili “Let's Encrypt”.
      • Cloudflare — registrujte besplatan nalog, dodajte vas domen, i dobijate SSL + CDN (brze ucitavanje) + DDoS zastitu (zastita od preopterecenja). Sve besplatno.
      • Bilo koji hosting — Let's Encrypt je besplatan SSL sertifikat koji radi sa bilo kim. Vecina hosting provajdera ima automatsku instalaciju.
      Proverite odmah: Otvorite vas sajt u browseru. Da li vidite katanac? Ako ne, to je vas prioritet broj jedan. Pokrenite nas besplatni skener za detaljnu SSL proveru.

      3. Bezbednosni headeri — uputstva za obezbedjenje

      Zamislite da ste vlasnik zgrade i angazujete obezbedjenje. Obezbedjenju dajete instrukcije: “Ne pustaj nikoga bez propusnice. Ne dozvoli nikome da unosi pakete bez provere. Ako neko kaze da je dosao po narudzbinu, proveri sa stanarima.”

      Bezbednosni headeri su upravo to — instrukcije koje vas server (zgrada) salje browseru (obezbedjenju) o tome kako da zastiti posetioce vaseg sajta. Bez tih instrukcija, obezbedjenje (browser) pusta svakoga unutra bez provere.

      Pet najvaznijih headera, objasnjenih bez tehnickog zargona:

      • Content-Security-Policy (CSP)
        Instrukcija: “Ucitavaj slike, skripte i stilove SAMO sa ovih adresa koje sam odobrio.”
        Bez ovoga: Neko moze da ubaci maliciozni kod na vas sajt koji krade podatke posetilaca.
      • Strict-Transport-Security (HSTS)
        Instrukcija: “Uvek koristi zakljucanu kovertu (HTTPS). Cak i ako neko pokusa da posalje razglednicu (HTTP), pretvori je u kovertu.”
        Bez ovoga: Napadac moze da prevari browser da koristi nezasticenu vezu.
      • X-Frame-Options
        Instrukcija: “Ne dozvoli da drugi sajtovi prikazu moj sajt u svom prozoru (iframe-u).”
        Bez ovoga: Napadac moze da stavi vas sajt u nevidljivi okvir i prevari posetioce da kliknu na nesto sto ne zele (clickjacking).
      • X-Content-Type-Options
        Instrukcija: “Veruj tipu fajla koji ti kazem. Ne pogadjaj sam.”
        Bez ovoga: Browser moze pogresno da protumaci fajl i izvrsi maliciozan kod.
      • Referrer-Policy
        Instrukcija: “Kada posetilac klikne link ka drugom sajtu, ne govori tom sajtu sa koje tacne stranice dolazi.”
        Bez ovoga: Drugi sajtovi mogu da vide tacne URL-ove vasih stranica, ukljucujuci osetljive parametre.

      Kako da dodate headere (po platformi):

      • Vercel — dodajte ih u vercel.json fajl. Detaljna uputstva u nasem Vercel bezbednosnom vodicu.
      • Netlify — napravite fajl _headers u root direktorijumu projekta i upisite headere.
      • GitHub Pages — GitHub Pages ne podrzava custom headere direktno. Resenje: stavite sajt iza Cloudflare-a (besplatno) i koristite Transform Rules.
      • WordPress — instalirajte plugin “Headers Security Advanced & HSTS WP” (besplatan) i ukljucite headere jednim klikom.
      • Cloudflare — idite u Rules → Transform Rules → Modify Response Header. Dodajte svaki header pojedinacno.
      Ne morate da pamtite ovo. Pokrenite nas skener — on ce vam tacno reci koji headeri nedostaju i dace vam gotov kod za copy-paste.

      4. Lozinke i dvofaktorska zastita (2FA)

      Lozinka je kljuc vase kuce. Dvofaktorska autentifikacija (2FA) je alarm — cak i ako neko ukrade kljuc, alarm ce ga zaustaviti.

      Pravila za jaku lozinku:

      • Minimum 12 karaktera — svaki dodatni karakter eksponencijalno povecava vreme potrebno za pogadjanje. Lozinka od 8 karaktera se probije za sekunde, lozinka od 16 karaktera zahteva milione godina.
      • Kombinujte velika slova, mala slova, brojeve i specijalne znakove (&, !, #, %). Primer: M0j@L1cna$ifra2026!
      • Nikad ista lozinka za dva sajta — ako jedan sajt bude hakovan, sve vase lozinke su kompromitovane.
      • Koristite password manager — Bitwarden je besplatan i pamti sve lozinke za vas. Vi pamtite samo jednu glavnu lozinku.

      Sta je 2FA i zasto je obavezna:

      Dvofaktorska autentifikacija znaci da vam za login, pored lozinke, treba i drugi dokaz identiteta. Najcesce je to:

      • Aplikacija na telefonu (Google Authenticator, Authy) — generise kod koji vazi 30 sekundi. Najbezbednije resenje.
      • SMS kod — dobijete poruku sa kodom. Bolje nego nista, ali manje bezbedno od aplikacije jer se SMS moze presresti.
      • Fizicki kljuc (YubiKey) — USB uredjaj koji ubacite u racunar. Najbezbednije, ali kosta oko 25-50 EUR.

      Dodatne mere za admin pristup:

      • Ne koristite “admin” kao korisnicko ime — to je prvo sto botovi probaju. Izaberite nesto unikatno.
      • Ogranicite login pokusaje — nakon 5 neuspesnih pokusaja, blokirajte tu IP adresu na 15 minuta. WordPress plugin: “Limit Login Attempts Reloaded” (besplatan).
      • Promenite podrazumevani login URL — umesto /wp-admin, stavite nesto kao /moj-pristup-2026. Plugin: “WPS Hide Login”.
      Brza provera: Da li vasa admin lozinka ima 12+ karaktera? Da li imate ukljucen 2FA? Ako ne, uradite to odmah — traje 3 minuta.

      5. Azuriranja i backup — vas sigurnosni pojas

      Zamislite vas sajt kao automobil. Azuriranja su redovni servis — sprecavaju kvarove. Backup je osiguranje — ako se nesto desi, imate od cega da se oporavite.

      Zasto su azuriranja kriticna:

      • Vecina hakovanja koristi poznate ranjivosti u starim verzijama softvera. Kada programeri otkriju problem, objave ispravku (patch). Ako vi ne azurirate, napadaci koriste taj poznati problem da upadnu.
      • To je kao da kljucar objavi da je otkrio manu u jednom modelu brave i ponudi besplatnu zamenu — a vi i dalje koristite staru bravu koju svaki lopov zna da otvori.

      Sta treba azurirati:

      • CMS (WordPress, Joomla, Drupal) — glavna platforma
      • Plugini/dodaci — svaki plugin je potencijalna tacka upada
      • Teme — vizuelni template vaseg sajta
      • PHP/Node/Python verzija — programski jezik na serveru
      • JavaScript biblioteke — ako koristite jQuery, React, ili slicno

      Backup strategija za pocetnike:

      • Automatski nedeljni backup — vecina hosting provajdera to nudi besplatno. Proverite u vasem panelu.
      • Cuvajte backup na drugom mestu — ako je backup na istom serveru kao sajt, hakovanje unistava i backup. Koristite Google Drive, Dropbox ili sličan servis.
      • Testirajte oporavak — jednom mesecno probajte da vratite sajt iz backup-a na test okruzenju. Backup koji ne radi je beskoristan.
      • WordPress preporuka — plugin “UpdraftPlus” (besplatan) pravi automatske backup-ove i cuva ih na cloud-u.
      Zlatno pravilo: Ukljucite automatske backup-ove DANAS. Ako se sutra nesto desi, mozete vratiti sajt za 10 minuta umesto da ga pravite ispocetka.

      How to Secure Your First Website

      A complete guide for absolute beginners — from zero to a protected site, no technical knowledge required

      1. Why security matters — even for a small site

      If you just built your first website — a portfolio, blog, or small business page — you might think: “Who would attack my site? I only have a few visitors.”

      The answer is surprising: automated programs (bots). Think of it this way — imagine a robot walking down a street, trying every door handle. It does not care whether it is a mansion or a studio apartment. If the door is unlocked, it walks in. Bots do the same thing on the internet — they scan millions of sites every day looking for unlocked “doors” (known vulnerabilities).

      What happens when a bot finds an unprotected site?

      • Google marks you as dangerous — visitors see a red warning screen before they can access your site. Your SEO ranking (position on Google) drops dramatically, sometimes permanently.
      • Your site becomes a spam tool — attackers inject invisible links to fake stores or malicious sites. Your visitors might not see them, but Google does.
      • Data theft — if you have a contact form, user accounts, or any personal data, all of it can be stolen and misused.
      • Reputation damage — once visitors learn your site was hacked, trust is hard to rebuild. It is like a restaurant getting a bad health inspection — even after fixing everything, people remember.
      • Recovery costs — fixing a hacked site can cost hundreds of dollars and days of work. Prevention is always cheaper than recovery.
      Good news: Basic protection is completely free and requires no programming knowledge. This guide covers everything you need — step by step, with plain English explanations.

      The visual flow of securing your site:

      Build site → Scan → Fix issues → Rescan → Deploy safely

      This guide follows exactly this flow. By the end, you will know precisely what to do and in what order.

      2. HTTPS and SSL — a locked envelope for your site

      Imagine sending a letter. You can send it as an open postcard — anyone who handles it can read the contents. Or you can put it in a locked envelope that only the recipient can open. HTTPS is that locked envelope for your website.

      What is SSL/HTTPS in simple terms:

      • HTTP (without the “S”) = postcard. Anyone on the network (your Wi-Fi, your internet provider, hackers on public Wi-Fi) can see what your visitors do on your site.
      • HTTPS (with “S” = Secure) = locked envelope. Communication is encrypted (encoded) so only the visitor and your server can read it.
      • SSL certificate = a digital identity card for your website. Like an ID card for your site — it proves you are really you, not someone pretending.

      How to recognize HTTPS: Look at the address bar (the bar where you type web addresses). If you see a small padlock and the address starts with https://, your site is protected. If you see “Not Secure”, HTTPS is not active.

      How to get HTTPS for free (by platform):

      • Vercel — automatic. The moment you deploy to Vercel, HTTPS is already active. Zero configuration needed.
      • Netlify — automatic. Same story — deploy your site and HTTPS is there.
      • GitHub Pages — go to Settings → Pages → check “Enforce HTTPS”. Done in 5 minutes.
      • WordPress — most hosting providers (SiteGround, Bluehost, etc.) offer free SSL with one click in cPanel. Look for “SSL/TLS” or “Let's Encrypt”.
      • Cloudflare — create a free account, add your domain, and you get SSL + CDN (faster loading) + DDoS protection (overload protection). All free.
      • Any hosting — Let's Encrypt is a free SSL certificate that works with anyone. Most hosts have automatic installation.
      Check right now: Open your site in a browser. Do you see a padlock? If not, that is your number one priority. Run our free scanner for a detailed SSL check.

      3. Security headers — instructions for your bodyguard

      Imagine you own a building and you hire a security guard. You give the guard instructions: “Do not let anyone in without a badge. Do not let anyone bring in packages without checking them. If someone says they are here for a delivery, confirm with the residents.”

      Security headers are exactly that — instructions your server (the building) sends to the browser (the security guard) about how to protect your site visitors. Without those instructions, the guard (browser) lets everyone in without checking.

      The five most important headers, explained without jargon:

      • Content-Security-Policy (CSP)
        Instruction: “Load images, scripts, and styles ONLY from these approved addresses.”
        Without it: Someone can inject malicious code into your site that steals visitor data.
      • Strict-Transport-Security (HSTS)
        Instruction: “Always use the locked envelope (HTTPS). Even if someone tries to send a postcard (HTTP), convert it to an envelope.”
        Without it: An attacker can trick the browser into using an unprotected connection.
      • X-Frame-Options
        Instruction: “Do not allow other sites to display my site inside their window (an iframe).”
        Without it: An attacker can put your site in an invisible frame and trick visitors into clicking things they do not want to (clickjacking).
      • X-Content-Type-Options
        Instruction: “Trust the file type I tell you. Do not guess on your own.”
        Without it: The browser might misinterpret a file and execute malicious code.
      • Referrer-Policy
        Instruction: “When a visitor clicks a link to another site, do not tell that site the exact page they came from.”
        Without it: Other sites can see the exact URLs of your pages, including sensitive parameters.

      How to add headers (by platform):

      • Vercel — add them to your vercel.json file. Detailed instructions in our Vercel security guide.
      • Netlify — create a _headers file in your project root directory and write the headers there.
      • GitHub Pages — GitHub Pages does not support custom headers directly. Solution: put your site behind Cloudflare (free) and use Transform Rules.
      • WordPress — install the “Headers Security Advanced & HSTS WP” plugin (free) and enable headers with one click.
      • Cloudflare — go to Rules → Transform Rules → Modify Response Header. Add each header individually.
      You do not need to memorize any of this. Run our scanner — it will tell you exactly which headers are missing and give you ready-to-use copy-paste code.

      4. Passwords and two-factor authentication (2FA)

      A password is the key to your house. Two-factor authentication (2FA) is the alarm system — even if someone steals the key, the alarm will stop them.

      Rules for a strong password:

      • Minimum 12 characters — each additional character exponentially increases the time needed to guess it. An 8-character password can be cracked in seconds; a 16-character password would take millions of years.
      • Mix it up with uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters (&, !, #, %). Example: My$ecure!Pass2026
      • Never reuse a password — if one site gets hacked, all your accounts using that same password are compromised.
      • Use a password manager — Bitwarden is free and remembers all your passwords. You only need to remember one master password.

      What is 2FA and why is it essential:

      Two-factor authentication means that to log in, besides your password, you also need a second proof of identity. The most common options:

      • Phone app (Google Authenticator, Authy) — generates a code that expires every 30 seconds. The most secure option.
      • SMS code — you receive a text message with a code. Better than nothing, but less secure than an app because SMS can be intercepted.
      • Physical key (YubiKey) — a USB device you plug into your computer. Most secure, but costs around $25-50.

      Extra measures for admin access:

      • Do not use “admin” as your username — that is the first thing bots try. Choose something unique.
      • Limit login attempts — after 5 failed attempts, block that IP address for 15 minutes. WordPress plugin: “Limit Login Attempts Reloaded” (free).
      • Change the default login URL — instead of /wp-admin, use something like /my-access-2026. Plugin: “WPS Hide Login”.
      Quick check: Does your admin password have 12+ characters? Is 2FA enabled? If not, do it now — it takes 3 minutes.

      5. Updates and backups — your safety net

      Think of your website as a car. Updates are regular maintenance — they prevent breakdowns. Backups are insurance — if something happens, you have something to fall back on.

      Why updates are critical:

      • Most hacks exploit known vulnerabilities in old versions of software. When developers discover a problem, they publish a fix (patch). If you do not update, attackers use that known problem to break in.
      • It is like a locksmith announcing they found a flaw in a particular lock model and offering a free replacement — but you keep using the old lock that every burglar knows how to open.

      What needs updating:

      • CMS (WordPress, Joomla, Drupal) — the main platform
      • Plugins/extensions — each plugin is a potential entry point
      • Themes — the visual template of your site
      • PHP/Node/Python version — the programming language on the server
      • JavaScript libraries — if you use jQuery, React, or similar

      Backup strategy for beginners:

      • Automatic weekly backups — most hosting providers offer this for free. Check your control panel.
      • Store backups somewhere else — if your backup is on the same server as your site, a hack destroys both. Use Google Drive, Dropbox, or a similar service.
      • Test your recovery — once a month, try restoring your site from backup on a test environment. A backup that does not work is useless.
      • WordPress recommendation — the “UpdraftPlus” plugin (free) creates automatic backups and stores them in the cloud.
      Golden rule: Enable automatic backups TODAY. If something happens tomorrow, you can restore your site in 10 minutes instead of building it from scratch.

      8. Five-minute security — prioritized action list

      Short on time? Here is a five-minute plan that covers 80% of all threats:

      • Minute 1 — HTTPS check: Open your site. Do you see a padlock in the address bar? If yes, move to the next step. If not, activate SSL on your hosting (usually one click).
      • Minute 2 — Password: Change your admin password to at least 12 characters. Use a mix of letters, numbers, and special characters. Save it in a password manager.
      • Minute 3 — 2FA: Enable two-factor authentication on your admin panel. Install Google Authenticator on your phone.
      • Minute 4 — Updates: Check if you have the latest version of your CMS and plugins. Click “Update All” if available.
